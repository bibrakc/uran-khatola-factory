### 1. Rear Spar Assembly
1. Break edges of HS-609PP with file or Scotchbrite wheel (see Sec E-E on DWG 3).
2. Smooth HS-609PP surfaces to 400-grit equivalent — remove all milling marks.
3. Final drill HS-603PP to HS-609PP: #30, cleco every 2nd–3rd hole, then fill in.
4. Drill/match-drill all holes **inboard** of HS-00006/HS-00005 rib attach points. **DO NOT** drill the rib attach holes or holes outboard of the bend line yet.
5. Remove HS-810-1, HS-814-1, HS-00003 from HS-702 channels. Taper ends of HS-810-1 and HS-814-1 (see Taper Detail on DWG 3).
6. Bend outboard ends of HS-810-1 and HS-814-1 (View B-B). Use wood blocks in vise + mallet. Check angle with cardboard template.
7. Mark bend line on HS-702 (HS-702 Front Spar Tab Detail).
8. Drill #30 relief hole 1/8" inboard of bend line, centered on flange bend radius. Flip spar, enlarge to 1/4" with unibit.
9. Trim inboard ends of HS-702 flanges (only halfway along the bend). Flatten remainder.
10. Bend tab with hand seamer or wood blocks.
11. Dimple HS-702, countersink HS-810-1 and HS-814-1 for the 4 flush AN426AD4 rivets at center (View A-A, flush heads aft).
12. Locate HS-708 to HS-603PP hole (8th from end of HS-609PP) — enlarge to #21.
13. Cleco HS-00716A doublers and HS-00717A hinge brackets to rear spar. Final drill #30.
14. Notch aft end of HS-00006 ribs to fit around HS-814 and HS-810 (HS-00006 Trim Detail). Make one left, one right.
15. Prepare HS-411APP and HS-411BPP around VA-146 bearing. Cleco to spar. Use HS-411BPP as drill guide for aft 4 VA-146 attach holes (#30). Remove, drill forward 2 VA-146 holes. Deburr, prime VA-146 if desired, rivet (–5 rivet).
16. Rivet HS-609PP bars to HS-603PP spar channels.
17. Rivet HS-412PP hinge brackets to rear spar.
18. Rivet HS-00717A hinge brackets and HS-00716A doubler to rear spar.
19. Bolt HS-411PP center bearing to spar (torque per Section 5.20).

### 2. Preparing the Ribs
1. Prepare HS-00006, HS-00005, HS-706, HS-707, HS-708 ribs — edge finish and flute (Sections 5.13 and 5.2).
2. Mark hole locations on HS-00006 aft flange (SEC D-D dimensions). Pilot drill #40.
3. Draw centerline on forward flange of HS-00005.
4. Enlarge HS-411 bolt holes to #12.

### 3. Drilling the Horizontal Stabilizer
1. Select left-side parts (HS-702, HS-603PP, HS-706, HS-707, HS-708). Cleco together.
2. Drill all HS-706, HS-707, HS-708 rib-to-spar holes to #30 (except HS-708 to HS-603PP).
3. Un-cleco, deburr, re-cleco.
4. Cleco HS-801PP skin.
5. Enlarge HS-708 to HS-603PP holes to #21.
6. Cleco HS-00003, HS-810-1, HS-814-1 to HS-702.
7. Slip HS-00006 and HS-00005 into place. Mark approximate hole locations on rib flanges using skin holes as guide.
8. Remove HS-00006 and HS-00005. Flute as needed between rivet hole marks.
9. Mark all rear spar assembly parts for reassembly.
10. Reinstall HS-00006 and HS-00005. Clamp HS-00006 to skin and spar.
11. Match drill #30 aft flange of HS-00005 to HS-603PP (use spar holes as guide). Remove HS-00005, clean chips.
12. Match drill #40 aft flange of HS-00006 to HS-702 and HS-00003.
13. Mark and drill #40 the two holes common to HS-00003/HS-702/HS-810-1 or HS-814-1 on forward side of spar bars (keep in line with HS-00006 aft flange holes — see SEC D-D).
14. Reinstall HS-00005. Align centerline to previously drilled holes. Clamp tight against HS-00003. Cleco aft flange to aft spar. Clamp top/bottom flanges to skin. Match-drill forward flange of HS-00005.
15. Drill to final size all remaining holes attaching HS-801PP skin. Suggested sequence: start at HS-708/rear spar intersection, proceed up rib and outward to tip. Cleco every 2nd–3rd hole.
16. Mark and disassemble all parts.

### 4. Preparing Parts for Assembly
1. Deburr all holes in skin and skeleton (Section 5.2).
2. Dimple understructure with squeezer. Dimple skin holes with C-frame tool.
3. Smooth all edges (Section 5.2).
4. Prime all parts as required (Section 5.1). **HS-609PP, HS-810-1, HS-814-1 are not Alclad — must prime.**

### 5. Riveting the Horizontal Stabilizer
1. Tape over holes that will attach HS-706, HS-708, HS-00005 ribs and HS-412PP brackets — do not accidentally rivet these before ribs are attached.
2. Rivet HS-609PP bars to HS-603PP spar channels.
3. Rivet HS-412PP hinge brackets to rear spar.
4. Rivet HS-810-1, HS-814-1, HS-00003 to HS-702 front spars. Remember flush rivets at center (View A-A and SEC D-D).
5. Rivet HS-00006 and HS-00005 to front spar assembly.
6. Lay HS-801PP skin (left side) on clean surface. Cleco then rivet HS-707 to top side.
7. Cleco HS-706 to skin. Temporarily cleco HS-708 to hold skin tight against HS-707. Rivet bottom side of HS-707 to HS-801PP. Remove HS-708.
8. Cleco HS-00716A doublers to rear spar.
9. Cleco ribs and spar assembly together.
10. Cleco forward spar assembly to HS-706, HS-707, HS-801PP. Cleco HS-708.
11. Blind rivet HS-702 to HS-707 and HS-708.
12. Rivet HS-702 to HS-706.
13. Rivet HS-702 and HS-708 to HS-801PP.
14. Repeat for right side. Cleco on rear spar assembly.
15. Rivet rear spar assembly, HS-00006, HS-00005, HS-706 to HS-801PP (all reachable with hand squeezer).
16. Blind rivet rear spar assembly to HS-708.
17. Repeat for right side.

## ⚠️ Known Gotchas and Warnings

### HS-00005 / HS-810-1 / HS-814-1 — Edge Distance (THE big one)
The front spar assembly is the most error-prone area of the entire empennage. The holes are not pre-drilled on the flanges — you drill them yourself by aligning parts. This is where edge distance violations happen.

**What goes wrong:** When you clamp HS-00005 and HS-00006 to the spar assembly and drill, if the parts shift even slightly, the holes end up too close to the edge of the rib flanges or the angle material. The minimum edge distance is 2× the rivet diameter from hole center to nearest edge.

**Prevention:**
- Clamp everything firmly before drilling. Use multiple clamps.
- Check edge distance on every hole before drilling to final size.
- Drill pilot holes (#40) first, check edge distance, then enlarge.
- The HS-00005 rib is particularly vulnerable because it sits between HS-00003 and HS-00006 with tight tolerances.

**If you violate edge distance:** Order new parts. Do not try to fix with a doubler unless you consult Van's. This is a structural area.

### Rivet Size at Front Spar Center
The 4 center rivets at View A-A are **AN426AD4-7** (flush, -7 length), not -6. Easy to grab the wrong length. Use your rivet gauge. The flush heads go **aft**.

### HS-00006 Notch
The aft end of HS-00006 must be notched to clear HS-810 and HS-814. Make one left and one right — they are mirror images. Do not skip this step or the rib will not seat properly.

### Fluting Before Drilling
Flute ribs **before** match-drilling to the skin. If you drill first, fluting will shift the holes and they will no longer align with the skin.

### Tape Over Rear Spar Holes
Before riveting the rear spar, tape over the holes that will later attach the ribs and hinge brackets. It is very easy to accidentally rivet these closed, which then requires drilling out rivets in a tight space.

### HS-00716A / HS-00717A (Rev 6 change)
If your plans are older, they may reference HS-413PP. The current parts are HS-00716A (nested spar doubler) and HS-00717A (hinge bracket). Make sure you have the correct parts from Van's.

### Rear Spar Misalignment
If the rear spar does not align after the skins are on, the fix is to stretch the front spar assembly slightly (loosen the 6-degree angle at the front spar). Do not force the rear spar — diagnose the root cause first.

## Builder Notes

**From Bibrak's build (RV-7 experience, now RV-8):**
- Edge distance issue on HS-00005 and HS-810/HS-814 — had to order replacement parts. Root cause: parts shifted during drilling. Fix: clamp more aggressively and check edge distance before every hole.
- Wrong rivet size at front spar center (AN470AD4-6 instead of -7). Drilled out and replaced. Get a rivet gauge before starting rivets.
- HS-707 rib leading edge protruded because rib was not fluted. Eventually fixed with larger rivet. Better fix: remove rivets on protruding side, flute, re-rivet.
- Rear spar misalignment fixed by stretching front assembly.

**From papalimabravo.com (Peter's RV-8):**
- Service Bulletin 14-01-31 compliance required additional work on the HS front spar area. Check if your kit requires this SB.
- Use C-clamps (not just cleco clamps) when drilling HS-00002 angles — cleco clamps are insufficient to hold alignment.
- For the front spar rivets that are hard to reach, an offset rivet set is needed. Have one on hand before you start riveting.

**From rv.squawk1200.net (Philip's RV-8):**
- Ruined HS-810-1/HS-814-1 on first attempt by removing too much material during tapering. Root cause: rough cuts were too close to the final line, leaving no room to ease in gradually. **Fix: make rough cuts well outside the line (1/4"+ extra), then work in slowly with belt sander and Scotchbrite.** The plans call for 1/4" radius on the ends — easy to go to 3/16" by accident.
- Dremel cutoff wheels are too hard to control for this taper work. Use a hacksaw for rough cuts, then belt sander + Scotchbrite to finish.
- HS-411PP center bearing: only one half (HS-411BPP) is pre-drilled. Clamp both halves over VA-146, cleco the assembly to the spar, then drill all three pieces together. Fuss over alignment here — this is a good opportunity to ruin a part.
- When match-drilling HS-412PP hinge brackets (only 2 holes each), you can only cleco one hole while drilling the other. Add a clamp for stability before drilling.
- Get a drill bit size gauge before starting. Knowing exactly which bit you have prevents stopping mid-session to order parts.
- Common mistake: drilling HS rib/skin holes to #30 instead of #40. If this happens, use AN470AD4-4 rivets (one size up) — structurally acceptable but cosmetically different.
- If ribs were drilled before fluting, check hole alignment carefully. Minor misalignment can sometimes be corrected with a slight ream.
