## Overview

The elevators are built much like the rudder — stiffeners back-riveted to skin, skeleton assembled, then skin wrapped around. The key differences:
- Lead counterweights forward of hinge line (balance requirement — critical for flutter prevention)
- Left elevator has a **trim tab** — the most complex part of the empennage
- Both elevators must be balanced after completion

Build the **right elevator first** — it is simpler (no trim tab). Use it to learn the techniques before tackling the left.

### 1. Trimming the Stiffeners
1. Cut E-720 stiffeners from pre-punched angle stock (same technique as rudder R-815 stiffeners). Double notch = overall length.
2. Trim flange to fit inside tapered elevator. Single notches mark trim start points.
3. Note: E-720J, E-720K, E-720L are made from E-720D, E-720E, E-720F (see Note 1, DWG 5). Set aside the extras for the left elevator.

### 2. Preparing the Elevator Skins
1. Drill stiffeners to E-701-R skin. Drill E-615PP to E-701-L (left elevator only at this stage).
2. Disassemble. Deburr, dimple, prime as desired.
3. Back rivet stiffeners to E-701-L/R skins (Section 5.6).
4. Back rivet E-615PP to E-701-L.
5. Bend trailing edge of elevator (Section 5.7). **Do not add RTV sealant to trailing edge until just prior to assembly.**

### 3. Preparing the Right Elevator
1. File radius on top and bottom edges of E-00001 outboard hinge doubler to nest against spar radius.
2. Cleco and drill E-610PP, E-611PP reinforcement plates, E-00001A & B hinge doublers, and platenuts to E-702 spar.
3. Prepare E-703 end rib and E-704 counterbalance rib (edge finish, flute — Sections 5.2, 5.13).
4. Cleco and drill E-703 to E-704, then cleco and drill to E-702. Remove ribs from spar.
5. Place E-714 counterweight on forward end of E-703/E-704 (View F-F, DWG 5). Cleco E-713 counterbalance skin over E-714. Step in front face of E-714 should butt against outboard edge of E-713.
6. Use two holes in forward face of E-713 as guide. Drill #12 for screws holding E-714. **Use drilling lubricant on lead E-714.**
7. Un-cleco E-713, remove E-714, re-cleco E-713 to E-703/E-704. Cleco E-703/E-704/E-713 subassembly to E-702.
8. Cleco E-709 root rib (right) to E-702. Final drill #40, E-709 to E-702.
9. Cleco E-701-R to understructure. Note: E-701-R lays on top of E-713.
10. Remove clecos connecting E-709 to E-702. Cleco and drill WD-605-1-R elevator horn to E-702 and E-709.
11. Drill E-701-R skin to understructure. Disassemble.
12. Dimple E-713 for screw heads attaching E-714 (Section 5.5).
13. Machine countersink E-714 for dimples in E-713 (Section 5.5).
14. Machine countersink E-709 attach holes on forward face of E-702 (flush heads forward — WD-605-1-R mounts flush).
15. Bevel inboard and aft edges of E-713 where it overlaps spar and rib flanges.
16. Deburr, dimple, prime as desired.

### 4. Riveting the Right Elevator
1. Rivet E-703 to E-704.
2. Rivet E-610PP, E-611PP, E-00001A & B, and platenuts to E-702.
3. Rivet E-709 to E-702 (flush heads forward).
4. Rivet E-703/E-704 to E-702.
5. Rivet WD-605-1-R to E-702 and E-709.
6. Rivet E-713 to E-701-R — two rivets top, two rivets bottom. (This eliminates need for blind rivets.)
7. Loosely place E-714 inside E-713 with screws partially inserted.
8. Insert elevator skeleton into E-701-R/E-713 — start at front end of E-703/E-704, rotate root end aft into position.
9. Cleco and rivet E-701-R and E-713 to understructure.
10. Finish attaching E-714.

## Build Sequence — Left Elevator

### 1. Preparing the Left Elevator
Same as right elevator steps 1–16, with these differences:
- Uses E-705 root rib (left) instead of E-709.
- Includes E-615PP trim access plate (already back-riveted to E-701-L in skin prep).
- Machine countersink E-705 attach holes on forward face of E-702 (flush heads forward — WD-605-1-L mounts flush).
- Machine countersink top flange of E-606PP for dimples in E-701-L skin. Dimple bottom flange of E-606PP.
- Machine countersink for rivets attaching E-705 to E-606PP.

### 2. Bending the Left Elevator Tabs (Trim Tab Cutout)
This must be done **before** the elevator is riveted together.
1. Remove vinyl from tabs and outboard of tabs where bending blocks will touch.
2. Lay square reference along trailing edge at trim tab cutout. Mark bend line perpendicular to aft edge. Offset bend line ~1/32" inboard to compensate for block movement during bending.
3. Fabricate bending blocks from particleboard/wood. Taper of inside block should approximate completed bend angle. Use double-sided tape between blocks and skin to prevent slipping.
4. Clamp skin and blocks to table edge. Bend bottom tab down using face of wood block, working progressively across tab. Finish with flush set and rivet gun at low setting — work across entire tab without stopping.
5. With block still clamped, bend upper tab up and over bottom tab. Note: upper tab overlaps lower so joint sheds water.

### 3. Riveting the Left Elevator
1. Rivet E-703 to E-704.
2. Rivet E-610PP, E-611PP, E-00001A & B, platenuts to E-702.
3. Rivet E-705 to E-702 (flush heads forward).
4. Rivet E-703/E-704 to E-702.
5. Rivet WD-605-1-L to E-702 and E-705.
6. Rivet E-713 to E-701-L — two rivets top, two rivets bottom.
7. Loosely place E-714 inside E-713 with screws partially inserted.
8. Insert skeleton into E-701-L/E-713 — start at front of E-703/E-704, rotate root end aft.
9. Cleco and rivet E-701-L and E-713 to understructure.
10. Rivet E-606PP to E-701-L along **bottom flange only** — leave top flange unriveted for trim tab hinge installation. Rivet E-606PP to E-705.
11. Finish attaching E-714.
12. See DWG 4 for trim system details. Install necessary parts while access is still available inside skin.

## Build Sequence — Trim Tab

### 1. Building the Trim Tab
1. Complete trailing edge bend of E-619-1-020 trim tab skin (Section 5.7).
2. Fabricate bending blocks (upper block hooks over lower to prevent slipping).
3. Mark bend lines on tab (Trim Tab Bend Detail, DWG 4). Offset ~1/32" from desired location.
4. Bend inboard end tabs (bottom down, then upper over bottom). Repeat for outboard tabs.
5. Cleco E-607PP to inside bottom surface of E-619PP-1-020.
6. Clamp E-718 to E-717 with clevis pin through attach hole for alignment. Cleco E-717 to trim tab skin. Using E-718 holes as guide, match drill E-718 to E-619-1-020 and E-607PP.
7. Position and clamp E-721 to top flange of E-607PP (per DWG 4 dimensions). Mark inboard edge of E-619PP-1-020 and E-607PP on E-721.
8. Drill remaining holes attaching E-619PP-1-020 to bottom flange of E-607PP. Drill holes attaching to top flange of E-607PP and E-721.
9. Disassemble trim tab.
10. Machine countersink top of E-607PP for dimples in trim tab skin. Dimple bottom flange of E-607PP.
11. Trim excess from E-717 and E-718 depending on trim system type (Manual or Electric, DWG 4).
12. Trim inboard edge of aft portion of E-721.
13. Prepare parts (edge finish, deburr, dimple, prime — Sections 5.2, 5.5, 5.1).
14. Cleco trim tab back together.
15. Rivet bottom of E-619-1-020 to bottom flange of E-607PP, E-717, E-718.

### 2. Installing the Trim Tab on the Elevator
1. Remove clecos along top flange of E-606PP. Clamp E-721 to upper flange of E-606PP and aft edge of E-701-L.
2. Adjust position so inboard and trailing edges of trim tab align with elevator. Trailing edge alignment is more critical than inboard edge. Use long straight edge.
3. Match drill and cleco forward portion of E-721 #40 using E-701-L and E-606PP holes as guide.
4. Mark inboard edge of E-701-L on E-721. Remove E-721, trim inboard end to match.
5. Re-cleco tab hinge and trim tab assembly onto elevator.
6. Sight down trailing edge with trim tab in trail. If tab has twist, correct it now — with clecos installed there is enough play to gently twist the tab. When aligned, tape or use second pair of hands to hold, then drill folded ends of inboard tabs.
7. With clecos in inboard tabs and reference marks for alignment, remove trim tab from elevator.
8. Carefully maintaining alignment, set rivets on top of trim tab.
9. Set blind rivets in ends of trim tab — ensure they do not interfere with rivets in inboard tabs of elevator.
10. Rivet forward (elevator) half of E-721 to elevator.
11. Install and bend trim tab hinge pin per DWG 4, View A-A. **Note: the hinge pin supplied is too short. You will receive longer pins with the fuselage kit.**

## Finishing the Elevators

1. Roll and rivet leading edges (Section 5.9).
2. Install rod end bearings (Detail D, DWG 5).
3. Check elevator travel — must swing through full up/down without interference (Section 15). It will probably be necessary to remove the bottom flange of HS-603PP to allow elevator horns enough swing. **Do NOT remove any HS-609PP bar.**
4. Align trailing edge "in trail" with stabilizer extended chord line. Counterbalance arm should align evenly with stabilizer. Secure elevator in this position.
5. Fabricate drill bushing (1/4" OD, 3/32" ID) to protect VA-146 bearing and guide drill for WD-605-1-R bolt hole. Pilot drill WD-605-1-R to #40 using bushing as guide. Remove elevator, drill to final size.
6. Repeat for left elevator.
7. **Counterweight balance:** At this point E-714 counterweights will overbalance the elevators. Final balance is done after painting. Leave counterweights slightly heavy, then drill small holes in inboard side until elevator balances "in trail."

## ⚠️ Known Gotchas and Warnings

### Left Elevator and Trim Tab — Most Mistakes Happen Here
Van's explicitly warns about this. The trim tab cutout, tab bending, hinge alignment, and trailing edge alignment all require careful attention. Do not rush.

### Trim Tab Trailing Edge Alignment
Sight down the trailing edge with the trim tab in trail. Any twist must be corrected before the rivets are set. Once riveted, it is very difficult to correct.

### Hinge Pin is Too Short
The hinge pin supplied in the empennage kit is too short to complete the installation. You will receive the correct length pins with the fuselage kit. Do not try to substitute — just leave it for now.

### E-714 Lead Counterweight Drilling
Use drilling lubricant when drilling lead. Lead is soft and gummy.

### Elevator Horn Bolt Hole
The drill bushing technique for locating the WD-605-1-R/L horn bolt hole is important. Without the bushing, the drill will wander in the VA-146 bearing and damage it. Make the bushing before you need it.

### HS-603PP Bottom Flange Removal
To allow full elevator travel, the bottom flange of HS-603PP may need to be trimmed. Do this carefully — remove only what is needed. **Never remove any HS-609PP bar material.**

### Counterweight Balance
The elevators will be overbalanced after assembly. This is intentional — you drill material out of the counterweight after painting to achieve final balance. A correctly balanced elevator stays "in trail" when released.

### Fiberglass Tips — Edge Distance
The elevator tips have very close edge distances near the end ribs. You may need to fabricate aluminum backing strips and epoxy them to the fiberglass to get adequate edge distance for the pop rivets. See papalimabravo.com for technique.

## Builder Notes

**From papalimabravo.com (Peter's RV-8):**
- Elevator tips: close edge distances near end ribs. Fabricate aluminum backing strips, drill additional holes in strips, epoxy to fiberglass for stronger bond.
- For pop rivets closest to trailing edge on tips: use hand puller with slow steady pressure, or use a smaller rivet.
- Torque and Loctite counterweight before installing tip.

**From 7echocharlie.com (Eric's RV-14A — same technique as RV-8):**
- **CRITICAL — Plans omission on elevator bottom skins:** The RV-14A plans omit instructions to rivet 6 holes in each bottom skin before the foam rib installation step. These holes become inaccessible after the foam ribs are installed. Van's confirmed this is a known omission. The RV-8 plans may have a similar issue. **Before closing the elevator, verify all skin-to-rear-spar holes are riveted.** If you miss them, the only fix is MK-319-BS blind rivets.
- Van's sent two right (-R) stiffeners instead of one left (-L) and one right (-R). **Check your stiffener inventory carefully** — the -L and -R labels are on the parts but may not be correct. Compare the actual geometry, not just the label.
- Elevator took 72.25 hours total (RV-14A). Expect similar on RV-8.
- Tank sealant used on foam ribs is very sticky — have acetone/MEK ready for cleanup.
- For elevator leading edge roll: tape pipe at equal distance from skin along entire length for even gap. 1" conduit gives better results than 3/4".
- After rolling, may need to massage skin slightly in middle section.
- Electric trim installed in left elevator — hook up linkage to keep trim tab from flopping during handling.
