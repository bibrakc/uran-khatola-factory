### 1. Drilling the Vertical Stabilizer
1. Cleco VS-808PP spar doubler to VS-803PP rear spar.
2. Cleco hinge brackets VS-410PP, VS-411PP, VS-412PP to rear spar (see Exploded Iso View on DWG 6).
3. **VS-410PP note:** The upper VS-410PP has two holes missing from the pattern. Use spar channel and doubler holes as drill guides and back-drill the entire 6-hole pattern through the **upper VS-410PP only**. The corner holes in the **lower VS-410PP** will be drilled later during fuselage assembly (DWG 78). Do not drill them now.
4. Prepare ribs VS-704, VS-705, VS-706, VS-707 — edge finish and flute (Sections 5.2 and 5.13).
5. Cleco ribs to front and rear spars.
6. Final drill #30: VS-808PP, VS-410PP, VS-411PP, VS-412PP to VS-803PP.
7. Drill all rib-to-spar attach holes to #30.
8. Cleco on VS-801PP / VS-901 skin.
9. Drill/match-drill to final size all holes attaching the skin.
10. Mark location and orientation of VS-803PP, VS-411PP, VS-412PP. Disassemble.

### 2. Preparing Parts for Assembly
1. Deburr all holes.
2. Dimple skin and understructure parts.
3. Machine countersink as required.
4. **Critical:** The lower portion of the rear spar (VS-803PP) must lay flush against the F-712/812 bulkhead when the VS is installed on the fuselage. Therefore the rivets in this lower region must be **flush on the forward side** of the rear spar. Machine countersink these holes — do not dimple them (see Rear View, SEC A-A on DWG 6, and Section 5.5). This is easy to miss and hard to fix after riveting.
5. Prime all parts as desired (Section 5.1).

### 3. Finishing the Vertical Stabilizer
1. Cleco VS-803PP to VS-808PP, VS-410PP, VS-411PP, VS-412PP together.
2. Tape over holes that will attach VS-704, VS-706, VS-707 ribs.
3. Rivet rear spar together — remember the flush rivets on the lower rear spar.
4. Rivet VS-704, VS-705, VS-706, VS-707 ribs to front spar.
5. Cleco on VS-801PP / VS-901 skin.
6. Rivet skin. Begin at intersection of VS-707 and VS-702, work toward tip. Then restart at same place and rivet along front spar toward root, and along VS-707 rib from front toward rear spar.
7. Cleco on rear spar assembly. Install remaining rivets along rear spar and end ribs with squeezer.
8. Blind rivet rear spar assembly to VS-707.

## ⚠️ Known Gotchas and Warnings

### Lower Rear Spar — Flush Rivets (Easy to Miss)
The most commonly missed detail on the VS. The lower portion of VS-803PP mates flush against the F-712/812 fuselage bulkhead. If you use standard shop-head rivets here (dimpled), the rivet heads will protrude and prevent the spar from seating flush against the bulkhead. You will not discover this until fuselage assembly — at which point you will need to drill out rivets in a very tight space.

**Fix:** Machine countersink the forward side of the lower rear spar holes before riveting. The plans call this out (Rear View, SEC A-A, DWG 6) but it is easy to overlook.

### Lower VS-410PP Corner Holes — Do NOT Drill Now
The corner holes in the lower VS-410PP hinge bracket are drilled during fuselage assembly (DWG 78), not now. If you drill them now you may get the wrong location. Leave them undrilled.

### Skin Dents
The VS skin (VS-801PP) is thin and dents easily. Handle with care. If you get a small dent, the Van's Air Force community recommends using a rivet gun with a flat set at very low pressure to work the dent out — do not use a hammer directly.

### Rivet Sequence on Skin
Start at the VS-707/VS-702 intersection and work toward the tip first, then restart and work toward the root. This prevents the skin from pulling in one direction and creating a twist.

### VS is Simpler Than HS
The VS is structurally simpler — one skin, fewer ribs, no center bearing. If you are waiting on HS replacement parts, this is a good time to get the VS fully assembled and out of the way.

## Builder Notes

**From papalimabravo.com (Peter's RV-8):**
- VS completed in a single 13-hour session with a helper. Priming, assembly, and riveting all in one day is achievable.
- Dent repair on VS skin: use rivet gun with flat set at low pressure, working progressively. Almost completely removes small dents.
- C-frame dimpling setup is important — get it dialed in before dimpling the skin.

**From aerovintage.com (RV-8 builder):**
- VS is about half the size and complexity of the HS. Good confidence builder.
- The one-piece skin wrapping around the skeleton is straightforward — cleco from the top and work down.
- Parts laid out and drilled in a couple of evenings.

**From rv-10.de (RV-10, same technique):**
- Straighten VS-808PP rear spar doubler before assembly — it can have a slight bow from manufacturing.
- Peel blue vinyl from skin inside only where you will be working on pre-punched holes.
