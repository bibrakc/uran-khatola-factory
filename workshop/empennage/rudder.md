### 1. Trimming the Stiffeners
1. The R-815 (A-H) stiffeners are snipped from pre-punched angle stock. Double notch = overall length. Cut center-to-center of guide holes with aviation snips.
2. Trim the flange (perpendicular to skin) to fit inside the tapered rudder. Single notches mark the start of trim cuts.
3. Clean up edges with file and Scotchbrite wheel.

### 2. Fitting and Riveting Stiffeners to Skin
1. Cleco stiffeners to R-801PP skin per their positions.
2. Drill all skin-to-stiffener holes to #40.
3. Mark stiffener positions. Disassemble.
4. Deburr holes, smooth edges, dimple, prime (Sections 5.2, 5.5, 5.1).
5. **Be careful deburring R-801PP** — it is 0.020" aluminum. Too much pressure will ruin a hole.
6. Rivet skin and stiffeners together. **Back riveting is the best technique here** (Section 5.6).
7. Bend rudder trailing edge (Section 5.7 — Folded Trailing Edges).

### 3. Building the Rudder Skeleton
1. Cleco R-606PP, R-607PP, R-608PP reinforcement plates to R-802PP spar. Note: R-606PP goes on the **forward (flange) side**, R-607PP and R-608PP go on the **rear**.
2. Fabricate R-717 spacer and both R-716 rudder bottom attach strips.
3. Trim R-710 (see R-710 trim detail on DWG 7).
4. Enlarge the bottom rod end hole in forward flange of R-704 to 3/8" with unibit.
5. Cleco R-704, R-405PD, R-710, R-717, and platenuts to R-802PP.
6. Prepare R-703 tip rib (flute, Section 5.13). Cleco to R-802PP.
7. Drill/enlarge #30 holes attaching R-703 to R-802PP. Remove R-703.
8. Cleco R-713 counterbalance skin on R-703. Flute forward flange of R-703 so the two holes in forward face of R-713 match those in R-703 forward flange.
9. Drill to final size holes attaching R-713 to R-703.
10. Drill to final size all pre-punched holes in R-802PP spar web.
11. Drill to final size holes attaching R-710 to R-405PD and R-704.
12. Cleco R-801PP skin, R-703 tip rib, R-713 counterbalance skin to understructure.
13. Clamp R-716 rudder bottom attach strips to assembly (Plan View and SEC D-D on DWG 7).
14. Match drill R-710 and R-716 strips to rudder assembly using R-801PP and R-704 holes as guides.
15. Drill remaining holes in R-713 and R-801PP to final size.
16. Mark and disassemble spar. Deburr all holes and edges (Section 5.2). Prime as desired (Section 5.1).
17. Dimple skins, spar, and ribs (Section 5.5).

### 4. Riveting the Rudder
1. Rivet R-713 to R-703. **Omit the 3 most aft rivets on each side** — these coincide with R-801PP skin attach. (R-801PP lays on top of R-713 when fully assembled.)
2. Enlarge the two 3/32" guide holes in forward web of R-703 to #12. Install E-614-020 lead counterweight. (E-614-020 can be removed and set aside during assembly to make handling easier — re-install before closing.)
3. Rivet R-703, R-704, R-405PD, R-606PP, R-607PP, R-608PP, R-717 to R-802PP.
4. Rivet R-710 to R-405PD, then R-704.
5. Before installing skin: place a fingertip-size daub of RTV inside the trailing edge at the end of each pair of stiffeners (Section 5.7).
6. Cleco R-703/R-713/E-614-020 subassembly, then R-801PP skin onto understructure.
7. Rivet R-801PP skin to understructure. For narrow spaces at aft ends of ribs: enlarge last 1–2 holes to 7/64" and install MK-319-BS blind rivets (same dimple as AN426AD3).
8. Add R-716 rudder bottom attach strips to R-704 bottom rib as you rivet the skin.

### 5. Completing the Leading Edge
1. The leading edge must be formed before the rudder can be installed on the VS.
2. Simply pulling the overhanging skins together creates a crease at the spar edge — use the rolled leading edge technique (Section 5.9).
3. Use a pipe or dowel to roll the skin smoothly. Blind rivets are used here — set with a hand pop-riveter. Make sure rivet heads are firmly against the skin before squeezing. **Blind rivets are difficult to drill out** — get it right the first time.

## ⚠️ Known Gotchas and Warnings

### R-801PP Skin is 0.020" — Very Thin
This is the thinnest skin in the empennage. Deburring too aggressively will enlarge or distort holes. Use light pressure. A sharp deburring bit, not a drill.

### R-606PP Plate Orientation
R-606PP goes on the **forward (flange) side** of the spar web. R-607PP and R-608PP go on the **rear**. Getting this backwards is a common mistake. Check the Exploded Iso View on DWG 7 before clecoing.

### Back Riveting the Stiffeners
Back riveting is strongly recommended for the stiffener-to-skin rivets. It produces a much flatter, cleaner result than gun-and-bucking-bar on thin skin. Set up a back-riveting plate (a piece of thick steel or a dedicated back-rivet plate).

### Trailing Edge Bend
The trailing edge must be bent before the skeleton is installed. Use the folded trailing edge technique (Section 5.7). Add RTV sealant at the end of each stiffener pair just before closing — this prevents the trailing edge from buzzing in flight.

### Lead Counterweight Drilling
When enlarging the R-703 guide holes to #12 for the E-614-020 lead counterweight, use a drilling lubricant. Lead is soft but gummy and will clog drill bits.

### Blind Rivets on Leading Edge
The leading edge blind rivets are difficult to drill out if set incorrectly. Make sure the rivet head is fully seated against the skin before squeezing. Use a hand pop-riveter (not pneumatic) for better feel and control.

### Pipe Size for Rolling Leading Edge
From rv8-hangar.com: use 1" diameter conduit for the rudder leading edge roll (not 3/4"). The larger diameter gives a smoother, more consistent curve. Tape the pipe at an equal distance from the skin along the entire length to get an even gap.

### Rudder Bottom Tip — Strobe Light Clearance
If you plan to install a strobe light in the rudder bottom tip, do not permanently install the bottom tip until you have the strobe and the tailwheel springs installed. Some builders have reported interference between the stock tip and the tailwheel springs. Trim the tip shorter if needed.

## Builder Notes

**From papalimabravo.com (Peter's RV-8):**
- For the pop rivets closest to the trailing edge on the tips: use a hand puller and apply slow, steady pressure. Alternatively use a smaller rivet in that location.
- Torque and apply Loctite to the counterweight before installing the tip.
- Consider making the VS tip removable (screws + nutplates) if you plan to mount a camera there.

**From 7echocharlie.com (Eric's RV-14A):**
- Van's has been known to ship wrong stiffener orientations. The rudder R-815 stiffeners are labeled A through H — verify the geometry matches the drawing, not just the label.

**From rv8-hangar.com (Michael's RV-8):**
- Rudder leading edge: tape pipe at equal distance from skin along entire length. This gives an even gap rather than a gap that widens from tip to root.
- After rolling, may need to massage the skin slightly in the middle section to get it perfectly flat.
- 1" conduit gives better results than 3/4" for the rudder.
